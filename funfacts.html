Myths and Facts About Cafe Culture

Explore the truths behind common cafe myths while enjoying your favorite beverages!

Myth: All coffee is the same.

Fact: Different coffee beans have unique flavors and aromas based on their origin!

Myth: Espresso has more caffeine than regular coffee.

Fact: Espresso has less caffeine per ounce but is served in smaller quantities!

Myth: Cold brew coffee is just regular coffee poured over ice.

Fact: Cold brew is steeped in cold water for 12-24 hours for a smoother taste!

Myth: The darker the roast, the more caffeine it has.

Fact: Lighter roasts often contain more caffeine because the roasting process reduces it!

Myth: All tea comes from black tea leaves.

Fact: There are various types of tea, including green, white, oolong, and herbal, each from different leaves!

Myth: Adding milk to coffee ruins its flavor.

Fact: Milk can enhance the flavor and make the coffee creamier and richer!

Myth: Decaf coffee has no caffeine at all.

Fact: Decaf still contains a small amount of caffeine, usually about 2-5 mg per cup!

Myth: All iced coffee is sweetened.

Fact: Iced coffee can be served black or sweetened, depending on your taste!

Myth: Latte art is just for show.

Fact: Latte art demonstrates the skill of a barista and enhances the drinking experience!

Myth: Coffee should be stored in the fridge.

Fact: Coffee is best stored in a cool, dark place in an airtight container to maintain freshness!
